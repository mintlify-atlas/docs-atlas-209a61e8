## Writing a Review

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Anime Page">
    Find the anime you want to review using search or your lists.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click Write Review">
    Click the "Write Review" or "Review" button on the anime page.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Overall Rating">
    Rate the anime from 1-10 using the star selector:
    
    ```
    1-2: Terrible
    3-4: Poor
    5-6: Average
    7-8: Good
    9-10: Excellent
    ```
    
    <Tip>
      Your overall rating is the most important metric. Be honest—it helps others and improves recommendations.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Rate Individual Aspects">
    Provide detailed ratings for:
    
    - **Story** (1-10): Plot, pacing, narrative structure
    - **Animation** (1-10): Visual quality, art style, fluidity
    - **Sound** (1-10): Music, voice acting, sound effects
    - **Character** (1-10): Development, personality, relationships
    - **Enjoyment** (1-10): Personal enjoyment factor
    
    <Note>
      Individual ratings are optional but provide richer feedback. The overall rating is calculated as an average if you don't set it manually.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Write Review Title">
    Create a compelling title that summarizes your opinion:
    
    Good examples:
    - "A Masterpiece of Storytelling and Animation"
    - "Great Concept, Disappointing Execution"
    - "The Perfect Comfort Anime for Cozy Weekends"
    
    Keep it under 100 characters.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Write Review Body">
    Share your detailed thoughts:
    
    **Recommended structure**:
    1. Opening hook (1-2 sentences)
    2. Story summary (no spoilers)
    3. What you liked
    4. What could be improved
    5. Who should watch this
    6. Final verdict
    
    <Tip>
      Aim for 200-500 words for a well-rounded review. Shorter reviews lack detail; longer reviews may lose readers.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Pros and Cons (Optional)">
    Create bullet-point lists:
    
    **Pros** (strengths):
    - Compelling character arcs
    - Stunning animation quality  
    - Memorable soundtrack
    
    **Cons** (weaknesses):
    - Slow pacing in middle episodes
    - Predictable plot twists
    - Weak supporting cast
    
    <Note>
      Pros and cons appear as highlighted sections in your review, making it easy for readers to scan.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set Review Metadata">
    Configure additional options:
    
    - **Watch Status**: Completed, Watching, Dropped
    - **Episodes Watched**: X out of Y total
    - **Contains Spoilers**: Check if review reveals plot points
    - **Tags**: Add relevant keywords (genre, themes, mood)
    - **Recommendation**: Yes, Neutral, No
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose Status">
    Save your review as:
    
    - **Published**: Live and visible to everyone
    - **Draft**: Private and editable later
    
    <Tip>
      Save as draft if you want to refine your thoughts before publishing. You can edit and publish anytime.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Submit Review">
    Click "Publish Review" or "Save Draft" to save your work.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Rating Categories Explained

### Story Rating (1-10)

Evaluate the narrative:
- **Plot**: Is it engaging, original, well-structured?
- **Pacing**: Does it move too fast/slow? Good balance?
- **Themes**: Are themes explored meaningfully?
- **Ending**: Satisfying conclusion? Loose ends?

### Animation Rating (1-10)

Assess visual quality:
- **Art Style**: Unique, appealing, consistent?
- **Fluidity**: Smooth movement in action scenes?
- **Detail**: Background art, character designs?
- **Direction**: Creative shot composition, cinematography?

### Sound Rating (1-10)

Rate audio elements:
- **Music**: Memorable soundtrack? Fits the mood?
- **Voice Acting**: Convincing performances?
- **Sound Effects**: Immersive audio design?
- **OP/ED**: Quality opening and ending themes?

### Character Rating (1-10)

Analyze the cast:
- **Development**: Do characters grow and change?
- **Personality**: Distinct, memorable characters?
- **Relationships**: Believable interactions?
- **Depth**: Motivations, backstories explored?

### Enjoyment Rating (1-10)

Your personal experience:
- **Fun Factor**: Did you enjoy watching?
- **Engagement**: Kept your attention?
- **Emotional Impact**: Made you feel something?
- **Rewatchability**: Would you watch again?

<Tip>
  Enjoyment is subjective! An anime can have technical flaws but still be highly enjoyable, or be well-made but not match your tastes.
</Tip>

## Review Examples

### Example: Positive Review

```markdown
Title: "A Masterpiece That Redefines the Genre"

Overall: 10/10
Story: 10/10 | Animation: 10/10 | Sound: 9/10 | Character: 10/10 | Enjoyment: 10/10

Attack on Titan completely exceeded my expectations from the first episode. 
The story masterfully blends action, mystery, and political intrigue...

**Pros:**
- Unpredictable plot twists
- Complex, morally gray characters
- Stunning animation during action sequences
- World-building that keeps expanding

**Cons:**
- Some pacing issues in Season 3
- Dense exposition in later episodes

**Recommendation:** Must-watch for any anime fan.
```

### Example: Mixed Review

```markdown
Title: "Great Visuals, Weak Story"

Overall: 6/10  
Story: 4/10 | Animation: 9/10 | Sound: 7/10 | Character: 5/10 | Enjoyment: 6/10

While the animation is absolutely gorgeous, the story feels shallow and
predictable. Characters lack depth beyond surface-level traits...

**Pros:**
- Breathtaking art style
- Smooth, fluid animation
- Great soundtrack

**Cons:**
- Predictable plot
- One-dimensional characters  
- Slow pacing

**Recommendation:** Watch for the visuals, but don't expect a compelling story.
```

## Spoiler Guidelines

<Warning>
  Always mark reviews as containing spoilers if they reveal:
  - Major plot points or twists
  - Character deaths or fates
  - Ending details
  - Surprise reveals
</Warning>

### Spoiler-Free Writing Tips

<Tip>
  Instead of revealing plot details, describe the *type* of story:
  - "A murder mystery with political undertones"
  - "A coming-of-age story about friendship"
  - "Dark fantasy with psychological horror elements"
</Tip>

You can discuss:
- General themes and tone
- Art style and animation quality
- First few episodes (common knowledge)
- Overall impressions
- Comparisons to other anime

Avoid revealing:
- Plot twists or surprises
- Character arcs and development
- Relationship outcomes
- Endings or conclusions

## Review Visibility

### Published Reviews

When published, reviews appear:
- On the anime's detail page
- In your public profile
- In community review feeds
- In search results (if tagged correctly)

### Draft Reviews

Drafts are:
- Only visible to you
- Accessible in My Reviews
- Editable anytime
- Can be published when ready

<Tip>
  Use drafts to:
  - Write reviews while watching (update as you progress)
  - Save partial thoughts to finish later
  - Get feedback before publishing
</Tip>

## Community Interaction

### Helpful Votes

Readers can mark reviews as helpful:
- Upvote button appears on published reviews
- Helpful count shown on review card
- Top reviews sorted by helpful votes

<Info>

## Review Tags

Add tags to categorize your review:

**Genre Tags**: Action, Romance, Comedy, Drama, Horror, Sci-Fi, Fantasy

**Theme Tags**: Coming-of-age, Revenge, Friendship, War, School-life, Sports

**Mood Tags**: Dark, Wholesome, Intense, Relaxing, Emotional, Funny

**Audience Tags**: For beginners, For veterans, Family-friendly, Mature

<Tip>
  Tags help readers find reviews relevant to their interests. Use 3-5 tags per review for best discoverability.
</Tip>

## Review Statistics

Track your reviewing activity:

- **Total Reviews**: Count of published reviews
- **Average Rating**: Your mean score across all reviews
- **Most Reviewed Genre**: Top category you review
- **Helpful Votes Received**: Total upvotes
- **Review Streak**: Consecutive days writing reviews

Stats appear on your profile page.

## Best Practices

<Tip>
  **Be Specific**: Instead of "good animation," write "fluid fight choreography with creative camera angles."
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Explain Your Ratings**: Don't just say "7/10"—explain what made it a 7 instead of an 8 or 6.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Consider Your Audience**: Who is this anime for? What should viewers expect?
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Balance Positives and Negatives**: Even great anime have flaws; even bad anime have redeeming qualities.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Update Old Reviews**: Rewatching an anime? Update your review with fresh perspective.
</Tip>
