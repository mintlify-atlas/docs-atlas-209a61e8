## Overview

AniDojo's list management system helps you organize and track your anime watching journey. Create custom lists, track episode progress, rate anime, and manage your entire collection in one place.

## Accessing Your Lists

Navigate to **My Lists** from the sidebar or go to `/my-lists` to access your anime collection.

```
Home › My Lists
```

## Understanding List Statuses

AniDojo organizes your anime into five status categories:

| Status | Description | Color Badge |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| **Watching** | Currently watching | Green |
| **Completed** | Finished watching | Red |
| **On Hold** | Paused temporarily | Yellow |
| **Dropped** | Stopped watching | Gray |
| **Plan to Watch** | Want to watch later | Blue |

## Adding Anime to Your Lists

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Add Anime">
    On the My Lists page, click the **+ Add Anime** button in the top-right corner.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Search for Anime">
    In the modal that appears, search for the anime you want to add by title. The search queries the MyAnimeList database.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Status">
    Choose the appropriate status:
    - Watching
    - Completed
    - On Hold
    - Dropped
    - Plan to Watch
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Details">
    Fill in additional information:
    - **Episodes watched**: Track your progress
    - **Rating**: Score from 1-10
    - **Start date**: When you began watching
    - **Finish date**: When you completed it
    - **Notes**: Personal notes (up to 500 characters)
    - **Tags**: Custom tags for organization
    - **Priority**: Low, Medium, or High
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save Entry">
    Click **Save** to add the anime to your list.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
You can also add anime directly from any anime detail page using the "Add to List" button!
</Tip>

## Organizing Your Lists

### Filtering by Status

Use the status tabs at the top to filter your collection:

- **All**: View your entire collection
- **Watching**: See what you're currently watching
- **Completed**: Browse finished anime
- **On Hold**: Review paused anime
- **Dropped**: See discontinued anime
- **Plan to Watch**: Check your watchlist

Each tab displays the count of anime in that category.

### Sorting Options

Sort your anime by:

- **Title (A-Z / Z-A)**: Alphabetical order
- **Score (High to Low / Low to High)**: By your rating
- **Date Added**: Most recently added first
- **Last Updated**: Recently modified entries
- **Progress**: By completion percentage

### Search Your Lists

Use the search bar to find anime in your collection by:
- Title (English or Japanese)
- Tags
- Notes

## View Modes

AniDojo offers three view modes for your lists:

### Cards View

Visual grid layout featuring:
- Cover art
- Title and status badge
- Progress bar
- Star rating
- Quick action buttons

<Note>
Cards view is perfect for browsing your collection visually.
</Note>

### Table View

Detailed spreadsheet-style layout showing:
- Thumbnail and title
- Type (TV, Movie, OVA)
- Progress (episodes watched)
- Your score
- Status badge
- Date added
- Action buttons

### Compact View

Condensed list format with:
- Small thumbnail
- Essential information
- Status indicator
- Quick actions

<Tip>
Table view is best when you need to see all details at once or manage many entries.
</Tip>

## Editing List Entries

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Quick Edit">
    Click the **Edit** button (pencil icon) on any anime card or in the actions column.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Update Information">
    Modify any of the following:
    - Status (move between categories)
    - Episodes watched
    - Rating (1-10 stars)
    - Notes
    - Favorite status
    - Priority level
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save Changes">
    Click **Save** to update the entry.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Quick Episode Increment

For anime you're currently watching:

1. Click the **+** button on the anime card
2. Increments episodes watched by 1
3. Automatically marks as "Completed" when you reach the final episode

<Info>
This feature makes it easy to track progress as you watch episodes!
</Info>

## List Statistics

View detailed statistics about your anime collection:

### Overview Stats

- **Total Anime**: Number of titles in your collection
- **Episodes Watched**: Total episode count
- **Days Watched**: Time spent watching (estimated at 24 min/episode)
- **Mean Score**: Average rating across all rated anime
- **Standard Deviation**: Rating consistency

### Status Breakdown

See counts for each status category:
- Watching, Completed, On Hold, Dropped, Plan to Watch

### Score Distribution

View how many anime you've rated at each score level (1-10).

### Top Genres

Discover which genres you watch most frequently.

<Tip>
Click the **Stats** button to toggle the statistics panel on and off.
</Tip>

## Importing and Exporting

### Import Lists

Bring your anime list from other platforms:

1. Click the **Import** button
2. Upload your list file (formats supported: MAL XML, JSON)
3. Review imported entries
4. Confirm import

### Export Lists

Backup or share your collection:

1. Click the **Export Lists** button in the Quick Actions panel
2. Downloads your collection as JSON
3. Includes all details (ratings, notes, tags, etc.)

<Info>
Regularly export your lists as a backup of your anime collection!
</Info>

## Advanced Features

### Custom Tags

Organize anime with custom tags:
- Add multiple tags per anime
- Filter by tags using search
- Create your own organizational system

Examples: "Favorites", "Rewatch", "With Friends", "Seasonal"

### Priority Levels

Set priority for your Plan to Watch list:
- **High**: Watch next
- **Medium**: Watch soon
- **Low**: Watch eventually

### Favorite Status

Mark anime as favorites:
- Toggle the heart icon
- Appears in your profile's favorite section
- Quick filter for your best anime

### Private Notes

Add personal notes (up to 500 characters):
- Remind yourself why you liked/disliked it
- Note where you left off
- Track thoughts for your review

<Note>
Notes are private and only visible to you.
</Note>

## Tips for List Management

1. **Update as you watch** - Keep episode counts current
2. **Rate after completing** - Add scores to finished anime
3. **Use notes** - Jot down thoughts for later reviews
4. **Organize with tags** - Create your own categories
5. **Set priorities** - Organize your Plan to Watch queue
6. **Export regularly** - Back up your collection
